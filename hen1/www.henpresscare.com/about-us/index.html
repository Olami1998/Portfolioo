Our Story

From Day One, We’ve Put People First

Back in 2010, a small group of local educators and social service professionals noticed a gap: too many families in our region didn’t have access to high-quality, personalized care for loved ones with developmental or physical disabilities. They believed rightly that every person has the right to build a life filled with purpose, dignity, and joy. So they opened the doors of what became HenPressCare, starting with a single client and three dedicated caregivers.

In the years since, we’ve grown carefully. Our team expanded to include licensed social workers, registered nurses, occupational therapists, and certified direct-support professionals. But one thing never changed: our commitment to building relationships first, then designing our programs around each person’s unique goals. That philosophy has allowed us to serve over one thousand clients across three counties, and we’re just getting started.

Our Mission

To empower individuals with disabilities through compassionate in-home care, skill building, and community connections so they can live as independently and joyfully as possible.

Our Vision

A world where every person, regardless of ability, belongs, contributes, and thrives in their community.

Our Core Values

Respect & Empathy: We treat everyone—clients, families, or colleagues—with kindness and understanding.

Excellence & Professionalism: Continuous training, evidence-driven practices, and strict safety protocols guide everything we do.

Collaboration: We partner closely with families, referral agencies, and community organizations to amplify impact.

Innovation: Whether it’s trying a new communication tool or updating our day-program curriculum, we’re always looking for better ways to help people succeed.

Initial Consultation (Free, 30 minutes)

We’ll meet you (in person or over video) to learn about your goals, challenges, and any medical or behavioral considerations.

Customized Care Plan

Our clinical team crafts an individualized plan covering in-home schedules, day-program options, community goals, and health needs.

Caregiver Matching & Training

We introduce you to one or two dedicated caregivers whose skills and personality best fit you or your loved one. All caregivers complete extra training—everything from sign language basics to adaptive cooking lessons.

Ongoing Support & Adjustments

We stay in touch with weekly check-ins, monthly team-meetings (family + clinical leads), and quarterly progress reviews. If goals change, we revise the plan immediately.

Graduation & Next Steps

When clients meet their goals—like successfully leading grocery shopping independently or joining a local art workshop—we celebrate. Then we set new milestones, because growth never ends.
